About Us

Computer Science for Kids (CS4K) is a student-run community service organization that provides UC Davis students the opportunity to apply their computer science knowledge and experience in the classrooms. CS4K strives to give every child an opportunity to learn computer science and to spark their interest in the field as a possible career path.

Volunteer

In CS4K, UC Davis volunteers are passionate about computer science and teaching. Our volunteers visit different schools in the Davis and Woodland communities, where they introduce the fundamentals of computer science to elementary and middle school students through programming tools, such as Scratch MIT, Swift Playgrounds, and more.

Frequently Asked Questions



Q1. Who can volunteer?

A. Anyone with a passion for computer science and teaching can volunteer! We are currently accepting new applicants! If you would like to become a volunteer, please apply here.

Q2. What will I do as a volunteer?

A. As a volunteer, you will teach weekly lessons to guide students on their project. These lesson plans will be provided to you, or you may create your own lesson plans.

Q3. Is it possible to receive units for volunteering?

A. Yes, students with 84 units or more are eligible to receive ECS 197T units towards graduation. Each unit requires 3 hours per week.

Q4. How can I partner with CS4K?

A. CS4K is always looking for new schools, sponsors, and organizations to collaborate with. If you are interested, please contact us here.

Q5. Where do I go if I still have questions about the program?

A. Please contact us here with your questions. A member from our team will respond within 2-3 days.
